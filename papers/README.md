# Downloaded Papers

## Core Papers — Internal State Probing

1. **The Internal State of an LLM Knows When It's Lying** (Azaria & Mitchell, 2023)
   - File: `2304.13734_internal_state_lying.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2304.13734
   - Why relevant: Foundational work on using hidden-layer activations to detect truthfulness. SAPLMA method achieves 71-83% accuracy.

2. **Detecting Hallucinations Using Semantic Entropy** (Farquhar et al., 2024, Nature)
   - File: `2302.09611_semantic_entropy.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2302.09611
   - Why relevant: Principled entropy-based confabulation detection. Distinguishes confabulations from other falsehood types.

3. **Discovering Latent Knowledge Without Supervision** (Burns et al., 2022)
   - File: `2212.03827_discovering_latent_knowledge.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2212.03827
   - Why relevant: CCS method extracts what model "believes" from internal states. Key for detecting know-say gaps.

4. **The Geometry of Truth** (Marks & Tegmark, 2023)
   - File: `2310.06824_geometry_of_truth.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2310.06824
   - Why relevant: Shows truth/false occupy geometrically separated regions in representation space.

5. **Representation Engineering** (Li et al., 2023)
   - File: `2310.01405_representation_engineering.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2310.01405
   - Why relevant: Framework for reading/controlling truthfulness via representation-level interventions.

6. **Do LLMs Know When They Hallucinate?** (Duan et al., 2024)
   - File: `2402.02181_llm_know_hallucinate.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2402.02181
   - Why relevant: Shows LLMs react differently in hidden states for genuine vs fabricated responses.

## Hallucination Taxonomy & Detection

7. **HACK: Hallucinations Along Certainty and Knowledge Axes** (Simhi et al., 2025)
   - File: `2510.24222_hack_hallucination_axes.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2510.24222
   - Why relevant: 2×2 taxonomy (knowledge × certainty). Identifies Certainty Misalignment — model knows but confidently outputs wrong answer.

8. **A Survey on Hallucination in LLMs** (Huang et al., 2023)
   - File: `2311.05232_hallucination_survey.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2311.05232
   - Why relevant: Comprehensive survey of hallucination types, causes, detection, and mitigation.

9. **HAD: Hallucination Detection** (Xu et al., 2025)
   - File: `2502.04427_had_hallucination_detection.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2502.04427
   - Why relevant: Comprehensive 11-category hallucination taxonomy with detection models.

10. **HaluEval: Hallucination Evaluation Benchmark** (Li et al., 2023)
    - File: `2305.11747_halueval.pdf`
    - arXiv: 2305.11747
    - Why relevant: Major hallucination benchmark with 35K samples across QA, dialogue, and summarization.

11. **MIND: Unsupervised Real-Time Hallucination Detection** (Su et al., 2024)
    - File: `2406.09569_mind_hallucination.pdf`
    - arXiv: 2406.09569
    - Why relevant: Unsupervised internal-state-based hallucination detection framework.

12. **HaluEval-Wild** (Zhu et al., 2024)
    - File: `2403.04307_halueval_wild.pdf`
    - arXiv: 2403.04307
    - Why relevant: Real-world hallucination evaluation benchmark from actual user-LLM interactions.

13. **Chain-of-Verification** (Dhuliawala et al., 2023)
    - File: `2310.18344_chain_of_verification.pdf`
    - arXiv: 2310.18344
    - Why relevant: Self-verification approach to reducing hallucinations.
